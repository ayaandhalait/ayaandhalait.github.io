About Me

Hey! I’m Ayaan, 13 years old and already spending way too much time convincing computers to do what I want. I build games, apps, and random software experiments that either solve a problem, teach me something new, or just make life a little more fun. Most of my projects start with “What if I could…” and end with me either celebrating a tiny win or screaming at a bug for an hour.

Being the CTO of the Student Initiative Board is a completely different adventure. It feels like being part scientist, part teacher, and part chaos coordinator. I get to organize projects, help others bring their ideas to life, and occasionally remind people that coding doesn’t work if you just stare at the screen and hope it does. It’s hectic, sometimes confusing, but always fun.

Outside of my official roles, I’m obsessed with exploring advanced tech like AI, quantum computing, and programming tricks that most people think are magic. I spend hours testing, learning, and sometimes breaking things just to see what happens next. I also enjoy contributing to open-source projects and collaborating with other young developers.

When I’m not coding or organizing projects, I’m probably tinkering with creative experiments, sketching ideas for new games, or teaching myself something new just because it sounds cool. Life is more fun when you’re constantly building, learning, and laughing at your own bugs along the way.
